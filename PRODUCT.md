# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who start projects and set them down: developers, writers, designers, and others with AuDHD or similar executive function patterns. Used privately, in quiet moments — returning to something after weeks or months, or deliberately setting something aside without declaring it abandoned.

Context of use: the user has chosen to return. They were not prompted. They arrive on their own terms.

## Product Purpose

Low-friction re-entry into paused projects. Drey captures ideas and projects, makes deliberate set-down a guilt-free act, and enables picking back up months later without shame and without reconstructing lost context.

The problem it solves is re-entry, not productivity. Success is that the user can return to something dormant and know what to do next, without the friction of reconstruction or the weight of implied failure.

Donationware. No revenue pressure, no growth obligations. Success = useful and finished.

## Brand Personality

Unhurried. Matter-of-fact. Plain.

Comfortable with silence. States things the way one states the date. Has no feelings about your projects and pretends none.

Warmth comes from restraint and respect — treating the user as a capable adult and leaving them alone — not from word choice or encouragement.

## Anti-references

- **Notion / productivity SaaS**: avoid the "calm but feature-dense" aesthetic — sidebars, toggles, database tables, the illusion of control through surface complexity.
- **Duolingo / Habitica**: avoid gamification entirely — streaks, celebration UI, progress bars, achievement icons, any loop that wants the user's continued attention.
- **Things 3 / Bear / linear task tools**: avoid the "minimal but still a task manager" framing — checkbox lists, priorities, due dates, anything that implies the user owes completion.

## Design Principles

1. **Hold, never reach.** Every design decision is tested against this: if the interface reaches toward the user (nudges, suggestions, counts, unrequested insight), it is wrong.
2. **Dormancy is the default state.** A space where everything is set down is a valid space. Activity is the exception. The UI must make stillness feel correct, not empty.
3. **One surface at a time.** Retrieval over inventory. The user asks; the space answers with one or a few. No full lists as landing states, no aggregate counts, no implied backlog.
4. **Putting-down is a first-class act.** Equally weighted with capture. It's where re-entry gets cheap — the set-down moment is the re-entry moment.
5. **The user's words, back to the user.** Never paraphrase, never interpret. Reflect verbatim. The app surfaces; the user concludes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA (4.5:1 body contrast, keyboard navigation, semantic HTML)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` throughout — motion is optional, never gating content
- Low cognitive load as a design constraint: few choices visible at once, no dense information states, no visual clutter
