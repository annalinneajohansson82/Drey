# Drey

Small, calm, donationware app for capturing ideas/projects, deliberately *setting them down* without guilt, and picking them back up later with low-friction re-entry. The problem it solves is re-entry: returning to a paused project months later without shame and without reconstructing lost context.

Not a business venture. No revenue pressure, no growth obligations. Success = useful and finished.

## Read first

- `docs/voice-and-principles.md` is the source of truth for soul, voice, vocabulary, and design principles. Defer to it over anything here if they ever conflict.

## The soul (non-negotiable)

**The app holds, it never reaches.** It holds your projects, holds your patterns, holds its tongue. Reaching includes nudges, suggestions, streaks, unrequested insight, and reassurance. If a feature reaches toward the user, it is wrong.

Three stances: assumes competence; no dopamine hunting; no opinions (including no reassurance). Confirm the action, add nothing.

## Hard constraints

- App never initiates. No notifications, reminders, nudges, streaks.
- Dormancy is the default healthy state in the data model; activity is the exception.
- Retrieval-first, never list-first as the landing view. No aggregate counts in view.
- Putting-down is a first-class action. Finish is opt-in and lives in a separate area.
- Local-first, exportable, plain-format storage. The user owns their data.
- Voice: no exclamation marks, no imperatives at the user, no praise, no reassurance, no motivational language, no diagnosis/inference about the user, no apology theater. Bare confirmations ("Saved." "Set down."). Time as location, not countdown. Reflect the user's own words verbatim.
